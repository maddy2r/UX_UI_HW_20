PROBLEM

We found that the usability of the current HSCO site lacks an overall cohesive experience for the user. It does not allow them to filter available pets or complete the adoption on the site.

USER RESEARCH

INTERVIEWS & FINDINGS

The goal of gathering surveys and conducting 1-1 interviews with pet parents was to understand their previous adoption experiences and find areas of opportunity.

Survey results from 12+ respondents showed 91% of adoption paperwork for rescue pets had to be completed in person vs. only 25% of respondents who bought from a breeder/3rd party.

This highlights an opportunity to improve Humane Society’s online adoption process.

DEFINITION

MEET ERIN ANDERSON & LEARN HER STORY

Based on competitor analysis, my team found that similar sized humane societies use PetFinder to host their pet listings. Since The Humane Society of Central Oregon does not currently use PetFinder, we jumped on the opportunity to design; a pet filtering feature as well as redesign the current pet information cards and digitize the adoption paperwork process.

I want to ADOPT not SHOP

TESTING

USABILITY AND IDERATIONS

I tested our defined features using our mobile design with 4+ users. The pet filter had a success rate of only 43%, I communicated this and other feedback to my team for iderations.

OUTCOMES

SOLUTION

We created a responsive website with visually engaging pet listings to encourage users to fill out and complete the adoption paperwork online.

RESPONSIVE DESIGN SOLUTION

My team and I created the website designed the mobile size first. Once iderations were made to our prototypes. I created the tablet size and a teammate created the desktop size.

LEARNING OUTCOMES

During my time leading the Research portion of this project, I learned how to extract our users experiences into shared habits to support our design decisions. Great user experience needs design backed by research. Working with this team taught me to be mindful of our project objective and timeline, as to not “boil the ocean”.
